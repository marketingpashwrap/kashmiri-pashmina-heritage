# Kashmiri Pashmina Heritage

Kashmiri Pashmina is more than just a fabric—it is a legacy woven through centuries of tradition, craftsmanship, and cultural identity. Originating in the Kashmir Valley, Pashmina shawls have long been associated with royalty, elegance, and timeless luxury.

## Origins of Pashmina

The journey of Pashmina begins in the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, where Changthangi goats grow a fine undercoat to survive extreme cold. This rare fiber is carefully collected and transported to Kashmir.

## The Art of Kashmiri Craftsmanship

What makes Kashmiri Pashmina unique is its entirely handmade process:

- Hand spinning of raw fiber  
- Traditional hand weaving on wooden looms  
- Intricate hand embroidery (such as Kani and Sozni work)  

Each shawl can take weeks or even months to complete.

## Cultural Significance

Pashmina has been an integral part of Kashmiri culture for centuries. It has been worn by royalty, gifted as a symbol of respect, and passed down as heirlooms across generations.

## Why Kashmiri Pashmina is So Valuable

- Extremely fine and rare fiber  
- Skilled artisan craftsmanship  
- Time-intensive production process  
- Deep cultural and historical roots
